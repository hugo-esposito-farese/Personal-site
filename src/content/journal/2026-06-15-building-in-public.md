I've been thinking about why I want to document this in public.

The honest answer: I want to be held accountable to actually building things, not just thinking about them. There's a version of me that spends years at 42, gets good at C, and graduates without shipping anything real. I don't want that.

Publishing forces a kind of clarity. You can't write "I'm building X" without actually building X.

But there's something else too. The builders I find most interesting — the ones whose work I actually follow — document the journey, not just the wins. Marc Lou writing about a product failing. DHH writing about why they built Rails. Paul Graham writing about his actual experience with Viaweb. The texture of the thinking matters as much as the outcome.

I'm not going to pretend I know what I'm doing. I'm a CS student who wants to build companies someday. Right now I'm learning how to build software and how to ship things people use. This site is the record of that.

Some of these projects will turn into businesses. Some will fail. I plan to document both.

The 42 AI Assistant is getting traction. Thirty students using it regularly now. I don't know if that's a business yet. I'm going to find out.
